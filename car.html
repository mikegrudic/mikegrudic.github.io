This Model

Among modern GTIs, this particular model has numerous advantages. The Rabbit Edition was produced only for the 2019 model year and this example is in the unique, head-turning Urano Grey color. Park of the Mk 7.5 generation, it gets the best of the differential, brake, and power upgrades of the Mk 7-7.5 GTIs. And the Mk7.5 generation came out after various teething issues in the earlier Mk7s were worked out -- its powertrain is generally agreed to be more reliable than the previous generations of gas-direct-injection turbo VW/Audi vehicles, and that has certainly been true for this example so far.

The Rabbit Edition trim sits between the S and SE in terms of equipment, having an upgraded lighting package and spoiler over the S, retaining the iconic Clark Plaid seats not found on the higher trims, and avoiding VW's notoriously-leaky sunroofs also found in the higher trims. It is modern enough to have a touchscreen with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, but comes before the Mk 8 generation's poorly-received interior user interface.

If you've been wanting to get into a modern GTI, this is the one.
